Launching into Enterprise IT Management Module

Unit 1: An Introduction to EIT

In Unit 1: An Introduction to EIT, I explored the foundational concepts of Enterprise IT (EIT) and how it differs from other trends like Business Information Systems and Industry 4.0/5.0. The focus was on understanding what EIT is, its historical evolution (EIT Eras), and key frameworks like the EIT knowledge tree. We discussed the importance of standards such as TOGAF and ITIL, along with bodies of knowledge (BoKs) like PMBOK and SEBOK, which help define the field.

By the end of this unit, I gained a clear understanding of what EIT is and its relevance in modern business. I learned how to explain EIT, identify key BoKs for EIT practitioners, and describe a transformative EIT component that has impacted business.

The lecturecast covered EIT definitions, technology eras, enterprise architecture, and the differences between traditional IT and EIT devices. We also touched on Industry 4.0 and 5.0, emphasizing automation and its role in business.

For further insight, the reading focused on an introduction to EIT, along with relevant standards and bodies, particularly the SFIA list of bodies of knowledge, providing a deeper dive into the standards governing this field.

Unit 2: EIT vs. Shadow IT

In Unit 2: EIT vs. Shadow IT, I explored the differences between formally sanctioned Enterprise IT (EIT) and the more informal, unsanctioned Shadow IT (also known as Stealth or Hobbyist IT). EIT typically follows formal processes like the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC), while Shadow IT is more ad hoc, often seen as more responsive and creative. However, Shadow IT can expose businesses to additional risks, such as cybersecurity threats.

This unit focused on understanding why some companies or departments adopt Shadow IT despite the risks and how businesses can evaluate its effectiveness. We discussed the balance between innovation and risk, as well as the potential benefits and drawbacks of using Shadow IT in an enterprise setting.

By the end of the week, I gained the ability to:

  • Clearly explain the differences between EIT and Shadow IT.
  • Understand why some companies opt for Shadow IT.
  • Evaluate the pros and cons of using Shadow IT within an organization.

Unit 3: EIT and Automation

In Unit 3: EIT and Automation, I delved into the relationship between Enterprise IT (EIT) and automation. The unit began by highlighting that automation is not a new concept, tracing its origins back thousands of years to the use of natural resources like rivers and wind for mechanical assistance. However, our focus was on the modern interplay between automation and computer systems.

I explored how computer systems have significantly enhanced automation, making processes more efficient and accessible. From early batch systems to contemporary programming languages, automation has been a cornerstone of computerization, digitalization, and digital transformation. The unit provided a historical overview of programming languages, discussed various programming paradigms, and showcased examples of functions and automation that these languages enable.

A key takeaway was understanding how automation simplifies business processes and digitizes manual tasks, thereby driving Industry 4.0 and 5.0 initiatives. By the end of the week, I was able to:

  • Describe scenarios where automated systems are beneficial.
  • Explain the criteria for selecting appropriate programming languages for automating business systems.
  • Discuss how automation influences elements of Industry 4.0 and 5.0.

Unit 4: A Comparative Study: Practical Automation Exercises using C and Python

In Unit 4: A Comparative Study: Practical Automation Exercises using C and Python, I engaged in hands-on programming exercises to implement a well-known data sorting algorithm using both C and Python. This unit emphasized the practical differences between compiled and interpreted languages and how these differences affect the performance and execution of algorithms.

I learned how to design and implement the same algorithm in two different programming languages, measuring and comparing their performance. The unit covered the fundamentals of programming in C and Python, including the history of these languages and their respective programming paradigms. Additionally, I explored how algorithmic optimizations can impact the efficiency and speed of applications.

A key takeaway was understanding the reasons behind performance differences between compiled and interpreted languages and how to optimize code for better performance. By the end of the week, I was able to:

  • Create a Python program that can sort a file of integers.
  • Compare the performance of my Python application with an equivalent C program.
  • Explain the reasons behind the performance differences between the two applications.

Unit 5: Business Systems, Outsourcing and the Cloud

In Unit 5: Business Systems, Outsourcing and the Cloud, I explored the evolution of business systems and the shift from traditional models to modern cloud-based solutions. The unit delved into the rise and decline of outsourcing practices, including off-shoring and near-shoring, and how the emergence of cloud computing has become a significant alternative.

I learned about different types of business systems and the historical context of their development through the four EIT Eras. The unit highlighted the pros and cons of outsourcing and its variants, discussing how businesses have leveraged these strategies to optimize operations and reduce costs.

A significant portion of the unit focused on Cloud Solution Providers (CSPs), examining the options and benefits of public cloud hosting and computing.

By the end of the week, I was able to:

  • Explain the various types of business systems.
  • Describe the advantages and disadvantages of outsourcing, off-shoring, and near-shoring.
  • Discuss the options and benefits provided by Cloud Solution Providers and how cloud hosting and computing can impact business operations.

Unit 6: A Cost Comparison Between On-premise, Private and Public Cloud Hosting

In Unit 6: A Cost Comparison Between On-premise, Private and Public Cloud Hosting, I examined the differences between on-premise, private cloud, and public cloud hosting solutions, focusing on cost analysis. The unit emphasized understanding the pros and cons of each hosting option by evaluating the setup and operational costs associated with a specific system.

I explored the technical and financial considerations involved in designing a cloud-based system versus an on-premise system. This included learning about the "lift and shift" process for migrating existing systems to the cloud. I gained practical skills in calculating and comparing the costs of hosting a system on-premise versus in the cloud, enabling me to make informed recommendations based on cost-effectiveness and business needs.

By the end of the week, I was able to:

  • Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of on-premise hosting compared to public cloud hosting.
  • Describe the methodology for calculating and comparing the costs of different hosting options.
  • Recommend the most suitable hosting solution for a given system, providing justifications based on technical and financial factors.

Unit 7: Software Licensing: Commercial vs. Open Source

In Unit 7: Software Licensing: Commercial vs. Open Source, I explored the critical topic of software licensing costs and their impact on modern businesses. The unit focused on comparing commercial licensing agreements with various open-source licensing options, analyzing the advantages and disadvantages of each approach.

I delved into different types of licenses suitable for various functions within a company, evaluating how an open-source approach can benefit business operations. The unit also addressed potential future challenges associated with emerging technologies and licensing models, such as digital rights management (DRM).

By the end of the week, I was able to:

  • Describe scenarios where commercial licenses are preferable and when open-source licenses are more suitable.
  • Discuss the pros and cons of different licensing approaches.
  • Evaluate the risks and challenges posed by emerging technologies and licensing models, including DRM.

Unit 9: Communications and Digital Transformation

In Unit 9: Communications and Digital Transformation, I explored the critical role of digital communications in driving the adoption of modern digital technologies. The unit highlighted Claude Shannon's seminal 1948 paper, which led to significant advancements in digitization and digitalization.

I studied the evolution of communication epochs and delved into pervasive computing and its application to the Internet of Things (IoT). By the end of the week, I was able to:

  • Explain how digital communications are integrated into industrial processes.
  • Describe various IoT technologies used in the industry.
  • Discuss how Business Process Modeling and Notation (BPMN) can be utilized to digitalize traditional processes.

Unit 10: The Digital Transformation Process

In Unit 10: The Digital Transformation Process, I focused on current techniques and approaches to digital transformation, specifically how to digitalize manual processes. The unit provided a practical demonstration of the digitalization design process, building upon previous designs and exercises.

By the end of the week, I was able to:

  • Create a digital transformation design using a popular design package.
  • Critique designs based on best practices.
  • Reflect on optimal approaches to tackling digitalization challenges within business processes.

Unit 11: Future Trends

In Unit 11: Future Trends, I explored the emerging trends shaping Enterprise IT, focusing on the impact of Artificial Intelligence (AI), Security, and Web 3.0. The unit encouraged critical thinking about which of these trends will have the most significant influence on EIT over the next five years.

By the end of the week, I was able to:

  • Describe key emerging IT trends.
  • Explain how these trends can affect EIT solutions.
  • Critically evaluate the potential effects of these trends on EIT systems.

Unit 12: The Great Debate

In Unit 12: The Great Debate, the final unit of the module, I participated in a collaborative debate to determine which of the four global trends—AI, Automation, Web 3.0, or Security—will have the most significant impact on Enterprise IT over the next five years.

By the end of the week, I was able to:

  • Articulate and justify my position on the most influential trend affecting EIT.
  • Create a persuasive presentation advocating for my selected trend.
  • Collaborate with peers to discuss and agree on which trend will have the greatest impact.

Completing this unit and the module as a whole, I reflected on the transformative learning experience, recognizing how the knowledge and skills acquired will equip me to address future challenges in the rapidly evolving field of Enterprise IT.
